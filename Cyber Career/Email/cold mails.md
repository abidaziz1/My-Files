## GENERAL COLD EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

### DO's:
✅ *Personalize every email* - mention something specific about the company/person
✅ *Keep it short* - 150-200 words max (people are busy)
✅ *Lead with value* - what can you do for them?
✅ *Include a call-to-action* - make it easy for them to respond
✅ *Proofread* - typos kill credibility in security
✅ *Follow up* - send a polite follow-up after 5-7 days if no response
✅ *Track your emails* - use tools like Streak or HubSpot to know when emails are opened

### DON'Ts:
❌ Don't send generic mass emails
❌ Don't write long paragraphs (use bullet points)
❌ Don't apologize for reaching out
❌ Don't just attach resume without context
❌ Don't be pushy or desperate
❌ Don't follow up more than twice
❌ Don't send on weekends (lower response rates)

### SUBJECT LINE TIPS:
- Keep under 50 characters when possible
- Be specific: "CEH Master Seeking Remote Pentesting Role"
- Avoid spam triggers: "URGENT," "Quick Question," excessive punctuation
- Test: "Mutual Connection: [Name]" if someone referred you
- Intrigue: "Impressed by [Company]'s Security Work"

### TIMING:
*Best times to send:*
- Tuesday-Thursday
- 8-10 AM or 2-4 PM in recipient's timezone
- Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons

### FINDING EMAIL ADDRESSES:
*Tools to use:*
- Hunter.io
- RocketReach
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Company website (often follows pattern: firstname.lastname@company.com)
- GitHub profile emails
- Use common patterns: first@company.com, firstname.lastname@company.com

### EMAIL SEQUENCE STRATEGY:

*Email 1 (Day 0):* Initial outreach  
*Email 2 (Day 5-7):* Polite follow-up  
*Email 3 (Day 14):* Final follow-up or different angle  
*After Email 3:* Move on or try different contact at company

*Sample Follow-up:*

Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on my email from last week about [topic]. I understand you're probably busy, so I'll keep this brief.

[One sentence reminder of your value proposition]

If the timing isn't right, no worries at all! Would you be willing to point me toward the right person to speak with?

Thanks!
[Your Name]

## TRACKING YOUR OUTREACH

*Create a spreadsheet with:*
- Company Name
- Contact Name & Title
- Email Address
- Date Sent
- Response Received? (Y/N)
- Follow-up Date
- Status (No Response / Interview Scheduled / Rejected / etc.)
- Notes

*Weekly goals:*
- 20-30 cold emails sent
- 10-15 LinkedIn connection requests
- 5-10 company applications
- Track response rates and adjust strategy

*Typical response rates:*
- Cold email: 5-15% response rate
- Referrals: 30-50% response rate
- LinkedIn InMail: 10-25% response rate

*Pro tip:* A/B test your subject lines and email formats to see what gets better responses!
